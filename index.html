About Me

Hey! I’m Daksh Kulkarni, a final-year B.Tech CS student who fell in love with building interactive worlds. My game-dev journey kicked off after playing Uncharted 4—the pacing, set pieces, and tactile traversal made me obsessed with how games create “feel.” That curiosity turned into prototypes, lab sessions, and lots of Unity scenes.


During my time at SRM University (AP) and a semester at UC Berkeley’s SCET, I focused on gameplay systems, XR interaction patterns, and playful UI. I enjoy fast iteration: graybox → input feel → moment-to-moment loops → polish. I’ve led and participated in game jams, organized lab events, and shipped projects spanning mobile, 3D, and mixed reality.


Interests: input-first design, feedback loops (camera shake / haptics / audio), networked transforms, and MR interactions that feel intuitive. When I’m not coding, I’m testing mechanics, tinkering with shaders, or sketching UX for the next prototype.
